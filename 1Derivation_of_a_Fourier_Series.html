Exponential Form:
\[ c_n=\frac{1}{2L}\int_{-L}^{+L}f(x)e^{-in\pi x/L}dx \] $$ f(x)=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{n=\infty} c_ne^{in\pi x/L} $$
Trigonometric Form:
$$ f(x)=\frac{a_0}{2}+\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}a_n \cos\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)+b_n \sin\left(\frac{n \pi x}{L}\right) $$ $$ a_n=\frac{1}{L}\int_{-L}^{+L}f(x)\cos\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)dx $$ $$ b_n=\frac{1}{L}\int_{-L}^{+L}f(x)\sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)dx $$

Derivation of Trigonometric Fourier Series
$$ f(x)=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{+\infty} c_ne^{in\pi x/L} $$ $$ \text{Multiply by } e^{-im\pi x/L}$$ $$ e^{-im\pi x/L} \cdot f(x)=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{+\infty} c_n \cdot e^{in\pi x/L} \cdot e^{-im\pi x/L} $$ $$ f(x) \cdot e^{-im\pi x/L} = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{+\infty} c_n \cdot e^{(i\pi x/L)(n-m)} $$ $$ \text{Integrate from -L to L} $$ $$ \int_{-L}^L f(x) \cdot e^{-im\pi x/L}dx = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{+\infty} c_n \int_{-L}^L e^{(i\pi x/L)(n-m)} dx $$
$$ \text{But due to orthonormality of complex exponentials:} $$ $$ \int_{-L}^L e^{(i\pi x/L)(n-m)}dx =\left\{ \begin{array}{ll} 2L \qquad \text{(if n=m)} \\ 0 \qquad \quad \text{(if nâ‰ m)} \end{array} \right \} = 2L \cdot \delta_{nm}$$ $$ \text{hence } \int_{-L}^L e^{(i\pi x/L)(n-m)} dx =2L\cdot\delta_{nm} $$ $$\text{And so:}$$ $$ \int_{-L}^L f(x)\cdot e^{-im\pi x/L}dx = 2L\cdot \sum_{n=-\infty}^{+\infty}c_n\cdot \delta_{mn}=2L\cdot c_m $$ $$ c_m=\frac{1}{2L}\int_{-L}^{+L}f(x)\cdot e^{-im\pi x/L}dx $$

Triangular Fourier Series Derivation
$$ f(x)=\left\{ \begin{array}{ll} \frac{-2A}{L}\cdot (x+L) \quad (-L < x < -L/2) \\ \frac{2A}{L}\cdot x \qquad \qquad (-L/2< x < L/2) \\ \frac{-2A}{L}\cdot (x-L) \quad (L/2< x < L) \end{array} \right. $$ $$ \text{First notice that f(x) is odd,}$$ $$ \text{hence } a_n=0 \text{ for all n,}$$ $$ \text{also, since the } b_{n} \text{ integrand is even about 0, } $$ $$ b_n=\frac{2}{L}\int_0^L f(x)\cdot \sin \left(\frac{n \pi x}{L}\right)dx $$

Square Function Fourier Series Derivation
$$ f(x)=\left\{ \begin{array}{ll} -A \quad (-L < x < 0) \\ A \quad (0< x < L) \end{array} \right. $$ $$ \text{Split the integration into two: From -L to 0 and from 0 to L}$$ $$ a_n=\frac{1}{L}\int_{-L}^0 -A\cdot \cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)dx + \frac{1}{L}\int_0^L A \cdot \cos \left(\frac{n \pi x}{L}\right)dx $$ $$ a_n=\frac{2A}{L}\frac{L}{n\pi} \sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)\biggr |_0^L=0 $$ $$ \text{This can be found more quickly by simply noting that f(x) is odd} $$ $$ \text{And so } a_n \text{ is 0 for all n.}$$
$$ b_n=\frac{1}{L}\int_{-L}^0 -A\cdot \sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)dx + \frac{1}{L}\int_0^L A \cdot \sin \left(\frac{n \pi x}{L} \right)dx $$ $$ b_n=\frac{-2A}{L}\frac{L}{n\pi} \cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)\biggr |_0^L $$ $$ b_n = \frac{2A}{n\pi}\left[1-\cos\left(n\pi \right) \right] $$ $$ \text{Note that } \cos \left(n \pi\right)=(-1)^n $$ $$ \text{hence } b_n=\frac{2A}{n\pi}(1-(-1)^n) $$ $$ f(x)=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{2A}{n\pi}(1-(-1)^n)\sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) $$

Sawtooth Fourier Series Derivation
$$ f(x)=\frac{Ax}{L} \quad (-L < x < L) $$ $$ a_n=\frac{1}{L^{2}}\int_{-L}^{L}Ax \cdot \cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)dx $$ $$ \text{f(x) is odd} $$ $$ \text{hence } a_n=0 \text{ for all n}$$ $$ b_n=\frac{1}{L^{2}}\int_{-L}^{L} Ax \cdot \sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)dx $$ $$ \text{f(x) is odd} $$ $$ \text{hence } b_n=\frac{2A}{L^{2}}\int_{0}^{L} x \cdot \sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)dx $$ $$ \text{Use integration by parts} $$
$$ u=x \qquad dv=\sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)dx $$ $$ du=dx \qquad v=\frac{-L}{n\pi}\cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right) $$ $$ b_n=\frac{2A}{L^{2}} \Big[ \frac{-Lx}{n\pi} \cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)\biggr |_0^L-\int_0^L\frac{-L}{n\pi} \cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)dx \Big] $$ $$ \text{Note that the second integrand is zero as it is of cosine over a full period.}$$ $$ b_n=-\frac{2A}{n\pi} \cos \left(n\pi \right)=-\frac{2A}{n\pi}(-1)^n $$ $$ b_n=\frac{2A}{n\pi}(-1)^{n+1} $$ $$ f(x)=\sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{2A}{n\pi}(-1)^{n+1} \sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right) $$

Dirac-Delta Fourier Series Derivation
$$ f(x) = \delta(x)=\left\{ \begin{array}{ll} +\infty \quad (x=0) \\ 0 \qquad (x \neq 0) \end{array} \right. $$ $$ b_n=\frac{1}{L}\int_{-L}^{+L}\delta(x)\cdot \sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)dx $$ $$ \delta(x) \text{ is an even function } $$ $$\text{hence } b_n=0 $$ $$ a_n=\frac{1}{L}\int_{-L}^{+L} \delta (x) \cdot \cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)dx $$ $$ a_n=\frac{1}{L} $$ $$ f(x)=\frac{1}{2L}+\sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{L} \cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right) $$

Quadratic Fourier Series Derivation
$$ f(x)=Ax^2 $$ $$ b_n = \frac{1}{L}\int_{-L}^{+L} Ax^2 \cdot \sin \left(\frac{n \pi x}{L} \right)dx $$ $$ \text{The function is odd around 0} $$ $$\text{hence } b_n=0 \text{ for all n}$$ $$ a_n= \frac{1}{L}\int_{-L}^{+L} Ax^2 \cdot \cos \left(\frac{n \pi x}{L} \right)dx $$ $$ \text{f(x) is even about 0} $$ $$ \text{hence } a_n=\frac{2}{L}\int_{0}^{+L} Ax^2 \cdot \cos \left(\frac{n \pi x}{L} \right)dx $$ $$ \text{ for } n=0 \text{ :} $$ $$ a_0 = \frac{2}{L} \int _{0}^{+L} Ax^2 dx $$ $$ a_0 = \frac{2AL^2}{3} $$
$$ \text{ for } n\neq 0 \text{ :} $$ $$ a_n = \frac{2A}{L}\int_0^L x^2\cdot \cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)dx $$ //this is a button example. Button is used to hide parts of the derivation which the students should be able to do, eg. integrate by parts. Makes the page neater.
$$ du = 2x\cdot dx \qquad v=\frac{L}{n\pi} \cdot \sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right) $$ $$ u=x^2 \qquad dv= \cos \left(\frac{n \pi x}{L} \right)dx $$ $$ a_n = \frac{2A}{L} \cdot \Big[ \frac{Lx^2}{n\pi}\cdot \sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)\biggr |_0^L $$ $$ -\int_0^L\frac{2xL}{n\pi}\cdot \sin \left(\frac{n \pi x}{L} \right)dx \Big] $$ $$ a_n = \frac{2A}{L} \cdot \left[0-\int_0^L\frac{2xL}{n\pi}\cdot \sin\left(\frac{n \pi x}{L}\right)dx \right] $$
$$ a_n = -\frac{4A}{n\pi}\int_0^L x\cdot \sin \left(\frac{n \pi x}{L} \right)dx $$ $$ u = x \qquad dv = \sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right) dx$$ $$ du = dx \qquad v = -\frac{L}{n\pi} \cdot \cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)dx $$ $$ a_n = \frac{-4A}{n\pi} \left[-\frac{xL}{n\pi}\cdot \cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)\biggr |_0^L - \int_0^L\frac{-L}{n\pi} \cdot \cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)dx \right] $$ $$ a_n = \frac{4A}{n\pi} \frac{xL}{n\pi}\cdot \cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right) \biggr |_0^L $$ $$ a_n = \frac{4AL^2}{n^2\pi^2}(-1)^n $$ $$ f(x) = \frac{AL^2}{3} + \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{4AL^2}{n^2\pi^2}(-1)^n\cdot \cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right) $$

Linear Equation Fourier Series Derivation
$$ f(x) = Ax $$ $$ a_n = \frac{1}{L}\int_{-L}^{+L} Ax \cdot \cos \left(\frac{n \pi x}{L} \right)dx $$ $$ Ax \text{ is an odd function} $$ $$ \text{hence } a_n=0 $$ $$ b_n = \frac{1}{L}\int_{-L}^{+L}Ax \cdot \sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right) dx $$ $$ b_n = \frac{2A}{L}\int_{0}^{+L}x \cdot \sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right) dx $$ $$ u=x \qquad dv = \sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)dx $$ $$ du=dx \qquad v = -\frac{L}{n\pi}\cdot \cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right) $$
$$ b_n = \frac{2A}{L}[-\frac{xL}{n\pi}\cdot \cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)\biggr |_{0}^{+L} - \int_{-L}^{+L}\frac{-L}{n\pi}\cdot \cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right) dx] $$ $$ b_n = \frac{2A}{L}\cdot [-\frac{xL}{n\pi}\cdot \cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)\biggr |_{0}^{+L} + \int_{0}^{+L}\frac{L}{n\pi}\cdot \cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right) dx] $$ $$ b_n = -\frac{2AL}{n\pi} \cos \left(n\pi \right) $$ $$ b_n = -\frac{2AL}{n\pi}(-1)^n $$ $$ b_n = \frac{2AL}{n\pi}(-1)^{n+1} $$ $$ f(x) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{2AL}{n\pi}(-1)^{n+1} \cdot \sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right) $$

Mod x Fourier Series Derivation
$$ f(x)=A|x| $$ $$ b_n = \frac{1}{L}\int_{-L}^{+L} A|x| \cdot \sin \left( \frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) dx $$ $$ \text{f(x) is even about 0} $$ $$ \text{hence } b_n=0 $$ $$ a_n = \frac{1}{L}\int_{-L}^{+L} A|x| \cdot \cos \left(\frac{n \pi x}{L} \right)dx $$ $$ \text{The function is even about 0} $$ $$ \text{hence } a_n = \frac{2}{L}\int_{0}^{+L} Ax \cdot \cos \left(\frac{n \pi x}{L}\right)dx $$
$$ \text{ for } n=0 \text{ :} $$ $$ a_0 = \frac{2A}{L}\int_0^L x dx $$ $$ a_0 = \frac{2A}{L}\frac{x^2}{2} \biggr |_0^L $$ $$ a_0 = AL $$ $$ \text{ for } n \neq 0 \text{ :} $$ $$ a_n = \frac{2A}{L}\int_{0}^{+L} x \cdot \cos \left(\frac{n \pi x}{L} \right)dx $$ $$ u=x \qquad dv= \cos\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)dx $$ $$ du=dx \qquad v=\frac{L}{n\pi}\cdot \sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right) $$
$$ a_n = \frac{2A}{L} \left[\frac{Lx}{n\pi}\cdot \sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)|_0^L-\int_0^{+L}\frac{L}{n\pi}\cdot \sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)dx \right]$$ $$ a_n = \frac{2A}{L} \left[0-\int_0^{+L}\frac{L}{n\pi}\cdot \sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)dx \right]$$ $$ a_n = \frac{2A}{L}\frac{L^2}{(n\pi)^2}\cdot \cos \left( \frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)\biggr |_0^L $$ $$ a_n = \frac{2A}{L}\frac{L^2}{(n\pi)^2}\cdot \left[(-1)^n-1 \right] $$ $$ a_n = \frac{2AL}{(n\pi)^2}\left[(-1)^n-1 \right] $$ $$ f(x)=\frac{AL}{2}+\sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{2AL}{(n\pi)^2}\left[(-1)^n-1 \right]\cdot \cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right) $$
