Derivation of Trigonometric Fourier Series
$$ f(x)=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{+\infty} c_ne^{in\pi x/L} $$ $$ \text{Multiply by } e^{-im\pi x/L}$$ $$ e^{-im\pi x/L} \cdot f(x)=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{+\infty} c_n \cdot e^{in\pi x/L} \cdot e^{-im\pi x/L} $$ $$ f(x) \cdot e^{-im\pi x/L} = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{+\infty} c_n \cdot e^{(i\pi x/L)(n-m)} $$ $$ \text{Integrate from -L to L} $$ $$ \int_{-L}^L f(x) \cdot e^{-im\pi x/L}dx = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{+\infty} c_n \int_{-L}^L e^{(i\pi x/L)(n-m)} dx $$
$$ \text{But due to orthonormality of complex exponentials:} $$ $$ \int_{-L}^L e^{(i\pi x/L)(n-m)}dx =\left\{ \begin{array}{ll} 2L \qquad \text{(if n=m)} \\ 0 \qquad \quad \text{(if n≠m)} \end{array} \right \} = 2L \cdot \delta_{nm}$$ $$ \text{hence } \int_{-L}^L e^{(i\pi x/L)(n-m)} dx =2L\cdot\delta_{nm} $$ $$\text{And so:}$$ $$ \int_{-L}^L f(x)\cdot e^{-im\pi x/L}dx = 2L\cdot \sum_{n=-\infty}^{+\infty}c_n\cdot \delta_{mn}=2L\cdot c_m $$ $$ c_m=\frac{1}{2L}\int_{-L}^{+L}f(x)\cdot e^{-im\pi x/L}dx $$

Triangular Fourier Series Derivation
$$ f(x)=\left\{ \begin{array}{ll} \frac{-2A}{L}\cdot (x+L) \quad (-L < x < -L/2) \\ \frac{2A}{L}\cdot x \qquad \qquad (-L/2< x < L/2) \\ \frac{-2A}{L}\cdot (x-L) \quad (L/2< x < L) \end{array} \right. $$ $$ \text{First notice that f(x) is odd,}$$ $$ \text{hence } a_n=0 \text{ for all n,}$$ $$ \text{also, since the } b_{n} \text{ integrand is even about 0, } $$ $$ b_n=\frac{2}{L}\int_0^L f(x)\cdot sin(\frac{n \pi x}{L})dx $$
