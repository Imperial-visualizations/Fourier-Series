Decomposition of a function into odd and even parts

Representing a function in terms of a trigonometric Fourier series is analogous to representing a vector in terms of basis vectors. Here sines and cosines are our basis functions, we have a method for finding the projection of the function in the direction of each basis function ($a_{n}$ and $b_{n}$ are the "projections" of $f(x)$ onto $\sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)$ and $\cos\left(\frac{n \pi x}{L} \right)$ respectively).



This visualisation shows how the first ten terms are introduced to the series, notice that usually as the number of terms included in the series increases, new terms become less significant (their amplitude decreases).
